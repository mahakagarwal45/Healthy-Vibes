MENSTRUAL CARE

DIET TO FOLLOW:

There is no specific diet that is recommended to follow during periods. .
However, some women may experience certain symptoms such as bloating, cramping, mood swings, and fatigue during their periods, and there are some dietary strategies that can help alleviate these symptoms.
1. Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water and fluids to avoid dehydration and bloating.
2. Eat iron-rich foods: Women tend to lose iron during their periods, so it is important to consume .iron-rich foods such as leafy green vegetables, lean meats, and beans to help replenish your body's iron levels. .
3.Increase your fiber intake: Eating high-fiber foods such as whole grains, fruits, and vegetables can help reduce constipation and bloating.
4. Limit caffeine and alcohol: Caffeine and alcohol can worsen menstrual cramps and mood swings, so it's best to limit or avoid them during your period.
5. Consider supplements: Some women may benefit from taking supplements such as magnesium, vitamin B6, and vitamin E to help reduce symptoms like cramping and bloating .

HOME REMEDY & PRECAUTIONS:

1. Heat therapy: Applying a heating pad or hot water bottle to your lower abdomen or back can help relieve menstrual cramps.
2. Exercise: Gentle exercise such as walking, yoga, or swimming can help reduce menstrual pain and improve mood.
